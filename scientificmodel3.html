Physical vs chemical changes
Physical changes are shifts in the composition of atoms that are reversible, and able to be undone in full by physical means. By contrast, chemical changes are irreversible shifts in the composition of atoms that cannot be undone by physical means. A common example of a physical change is the evaporation of water, where the water undergoes a stage change from liquid to gas, only to return to liquid form as precipitation. This can be seen when broiling water in a covered container, the water evaporates to the top of the container where it reforms as water droplets and returns to the base of the container in liquid form. A common chemical change is the burning of a candle, with the candle releasing carbon dioxide into the air during combustion and leaving behind a chemically altered wax remains. This cannot be reversed without chemical means (carbon capture) and is therefore a chemical change.

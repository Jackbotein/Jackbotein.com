About Justice Botein
Jack Botein is a justice on The International Court of Justice at the Hague. Following his graduation from undergraduate college, he consulted at various multinational firms in the Middle East. At a street corner in Riyadh he bumped into an foreign worker named Khoury, who was being beaten and denied compensation by his employer because he was a Shia Muslim. Justice Botein decided to quit his lucrative placement with the oppressive Saudi government, and instead helped Khoury get to safety abroad. This experience pushed Justice Botein to attend Yale Law School (‘85), where he concentrated in international and human rights law. From there, he went on to practice human rights law at The Skadden, Arps, Slate, Meagher & Flom Law Firm, where he notably represented The Republic of Nicaragua in Nicaragua v. United States, a landmark case in which Nicraragua sued the United States for undermining their democracy by supporting the Contras rebel group. In 2011, The United Nations (UN) General Assembly and Security Council both unanimously confirmed Justice Botein to the International Court of Justice. American Ambassador to the UN Susan Rice stated on the General Assembly Floor “I have known Justice Botein since our shared time at Skadden, and I am confident he will adjudicate all matters [at The ICJ] in a fair, compassionate and morally sound manner”. Justice Botein resides in the Mauritskade neighborhood of the Hague, he is 63 years old.

Parts of an atom: proton, neutron, electron, nucleus
An atom consists of multiple layers, akin to an onion. The innermost layer is called the nucleus, housing the protons and neutrons.ยน The protons have a positive charge, while the neutrons possess a neutral charge. This onion heart is surrounded by electron shells, three rings of electrons that surround the nucleus. These shells contain electrons which carry a negative charge. The further a ring of electrons in from the nucleus, the greater the number of electrons they hold, with the innermost shell containing two electrons while the outermost shell contains eight. The relationship between these negatively charged electrons and positively charged protons affects whether atoms will be stable or unstable and balanced or unbalanced.

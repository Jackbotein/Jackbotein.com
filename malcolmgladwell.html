        Thursday’s warmth set a record in Waterloo Region

        WR
        By Waterloo Region Record
        Fri., Dec. 17, 2021timer1 min. read

        WATERLOO REGION — Thursday’s unusual springlike warmth set a record for Waterloo Region, according to Environment Canada.

        The weather agency said temperature in the region hit a high of 14.4 C Thursday, breaking the previous record of 14.2 C set in 1984. Record-keeping began in 1914.

        A strong low pressure system tracked across Ontario on Thursday brought both strong winds and unseasonably warm temperatures for much of southern Ontario, the agency said.

        It was unusually windy as well. Peak wind gusts were recorded at 84 km/h at the Region of Waterloo International Airport on Thursday, very similar to strong wind gusts recorded across southern Ontario.

        Thursday was as warm as 17 C in Toronto and 17.8 C in Muskoka, two other Ontario cities where previous records were broken.

        This week’s brief springlike feeling ended abruptly on Friday as temperatures dropped.

        Temperatures are expected to dip a bit more this weekend as well with the return of flurries and periods of snow on Saturday and Sunday.

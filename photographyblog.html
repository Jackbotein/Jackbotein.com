Project #1: SomethingFromNothing
12/7/20
The SomethingFromNothing assignment was a great learning experience for me. I took photographs in two shoots, Earth on fire and Apple, Apple. I began this project with the idea that a great photo was not nessasarily contingent on framing lines and shapes. After examining a bunch of my photographs with Ms. Patel, I came to the conclusion that a great photo IS dependent on effectively capturing lines and shapes. I applied this my second photoshoot (apple, apple), where I made sure to frame my shots in a way that highlighted lines and shapes. This still remains an area of growth for me as a photographer, and I will continue to look at how I can best showcase photography locations in the future. Something that worked well in my two photoshoots was utlizing of shaddow and reflection, notably in image #1 where I was able to get the reflection of the fire onto the globe. Overall, I really enjoyed the SomethingFromNothing assignment, and I will continue applying the lessons I have learned from this exersize in future photoshoots. Two technical + creative concept questions about photography:
1. When framing a photo, what is the first thing you look for?
2. What features in Photoshop do you find yourself most commonly utilizing? What type of photograph is this feature best suited for?




This photograph jumped out at me for three core reasons. First, the action is in the center of the frame, with the soccer ball airborne, and the athlete with a green shirt looking directly at the center of the camera. Second, the background is blurred and the foreground (players + ball) are enhanced, making the whole photo appear more dynamic and bright that an unedited still. Third, the brightly lit time clock is visible in the frame, drawing one's eye.
